Jalebi

Description:

The south Asian variety is made by deep-frying maida flour (plain flour or all-purpose flour) batter in pretzel or circular shapes, which are then soaked in sugar syrup. Jalebi is eaten with curd or rabri (in North India) along with optional other flavors such as kewra (scented water).

Ingredients:

  • 3 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 cup hung curd
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 3 cup sugar
  • 5 strand saffron
  • 1/2 teaspoon powdered green cardamom
  • 1/2 cup corn flour
  • 1 1/2 pinch baking soda
  • 2 cup sunflower oil
  • 3 cup water
  • 4 drops rose essence
  • 1/2 teaspoon edible food color

Steps:

  • Prepare the Jalebi batter and let it ferment overnight.
  • Deep fry the Jalebis.
  • Soak the Jalebis in sugar syrup and serve.
